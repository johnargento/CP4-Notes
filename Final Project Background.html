1) Kanye Omari West was born in Atlanta, Georgia. His father was named Ray West and he was a photojournalist while his mother, Donda West was a teacher/professor who taught English at Chicago State University. Donda then became her sons manager until she passed away at the age of 58 due to a heart attack in 2007. Kanye's parents divorced when he was only 3 years old, he was then raised on Chicago's South Shore with his mother and spent the summer with his father. At the age of 10, Kanye West moved to China for a year with Donda while she was teaching there for a university-exchange program. When they had returned to Chicago, Kanye was drawn to hip-hop and became friends with the DJ and producer "No I.D." who later became his mentor in music. He graduated from Polaris High School and earned a scholarship to the Academy of Arts in Chicago but soon dropped out of college alltogether to pursue his interest in music.
2) After working with a few local artists and producers, Kanye developped a style called "Chipmunk Soul", which is where he speeds up soul samples. He moved to New York City in 2001 where he met artists such as; Jay-Z, Mos Def, Kweli, Ludacris, Beyonce and Alicia Keys. Although he was making some name for himself, he wasn't happy enough with being "backstage", West wanted to be the star. But, Kanye was not initially taken seriously, he was a middle-class art student that wore pink polos and Gucci.
